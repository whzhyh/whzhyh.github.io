  • Jenny has lost her way home. She was standing 25 yards away from her house, in the south-west direction. She walks 20 yards north and reaches point ‘A’. How far and in which direction would she have to read her house?

    • [ ] 20 yards, East
    • [ ] 15 yards, East
    • [x] 15 yards, West
    • [ ] 20 yards, West

  • A man jogs 4 miles towards east, then 8 miles towards north and from there he goes 2 miles towards west. In which direction is he, from his starting point?

    • [ ] East
    • [x] North-east
    • [ ] North-west
    • [ ] West

  • A - B means A plus B.
    A # B means A multiplied with B.
    A/B means A is greater than or equal to B.
    A ? B means A is less than B.
    On the basis of this information, and assuming that the given statements are true, find out which of the two conclusions (I and II) is/are definitely true.

    Statements:
    (V # X)/(V - X), X ? Y and Z/Y
    Conclusions:
    I. X ? Z
    II. (V - X) ? (V # X)

    • [x] Only conclusion I is true
    • [ ] Only conclusion II is true
    • [ ] Both the conclusions are true
    • [ ] Neither conclusion I nor II is true

  • “%” denotes “greater than”
    “>” denotes “equal to”
    “=” denotes “not less than”
    “@” denotes “not equal to”
    “#” denotes “less than”
    “*” denotes “not greater than”
    On the basis of this information, and assuming that the given statements are true, find out which of the two conclusions (I and II) is/are definitely true.

    Statements:
    P>S, S@T, P#R
    Conclusions:
    I. S%R
    II. P@T

    • [ ] Only conclusion I is true
    • [x] Only conclusion II is true
    • [ ] Neither conclusion I nor II is true
    • [ ] Both the conclusions are true
  • It has been proven by research that in India, a company which purchases saturation radio advertising will get maximum brand recognition.

    • [ ] For maximum brand recognition, a company need not spend on media channels other than radio publicizing
    • [ ] Brand recognition in India is more heavily dependent on where the brand advertises than the quality of its offering
  • Here, you will see a question followed by two statements I and II. Find out if the information given in the statement(s) is sufficient to answer the question.

  • There are certain conditions given in the passage area. A decision has to be taken on the basis of the given conditions. There are two cases based on the same set of condition. Take a decision for both the cases and mark the correct answer for each.
    The following are the details of the procedure for deciding delivery charges for goods bought from ABC company. The customers:

    1. are divided into two categories: Those who have sales region code of 10 or above into one category and those with a code less than 10 into another.
    2. must have bought goods worth $500 or more in the previous month.
    3. must not have dealership of any other similar company.
    4. must not have availed bulk discount before.
    5. must have been provided a special discount of 5% or less than that in the previous dealings.
    6. must have been regularly ordering for more than 3 years.

    However,

    1. if the customer fulfills all the conditions except (2), and if the sales region code is less than 10, delivery charges of $10 would be levied. Delivery charges of $8 would be levied for a code more than 10.
    2. if the candidate fulfills all the conditions except (3), and if the sales region code is less than 10, delivery charges of $5 would be levied. Delivery charges of $12
    3. if the customer does not fulfill 2 or more of the conditions stated above, then he/she would have to pay delivery charges of $30 irrespective of the sales region code.

      • [ ] The customer would have to pay $12 as delivery charges
      • [ ] Data insufficient
    2. Jacob is a customer whose sales region code is 14. He had bought goods worth $150 from ABC Company in June. He does not have dealership of any other similar company. He has never been provided any bulk discount or special discount.
      • [ ] The customer need not pay any delivery charges

  • Conditions for appointing a distributor, for petroleum gas throughout Georgia, are as follows. The applicant should:

    1. be an American by nationality.
    2. be in the age group of 21-50 years as on 5th September, 2008.

    7. not have any close relative as a dealer/distributor of any oil company.

      • [ ] Applicant should be selected

  • Consider the direction codes given below:

  • Sales drives in big organizations, many a times, fall flat on the face. A research showed that an average buyer remembers only 20% of the things discussed during a sales call. The saddest part is that the sales team doesn’t get to choose what those 20% of things would be. The world today is cluttered with information and thus it is essential that the sales team represents their product/service in the best possible manner. It is like answering questions that children ask. Expect basic and out of context questions and reply to each one of them. patiently, in a way that the customers understand the intricacies. You can use technical terms to explain your product and its features. No doubt, it will be an accurate methodology but certainly not the right one. Simplify your message and see how well your client remembers you and your presentation when you meet hime to finally close the deal.

    • [ ] A regular buyer would remember more than 20% of the details after a sales meeting.
    • [ ] A customer is as gullible as a child and hence may ask many questions.
    • [x] A better sales person would be the one who is able to explain the features of his/her product in a simple manner.
    • [ ] If you simplify your message, the customer would remember your entire presentation.
